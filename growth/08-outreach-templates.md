Each post would be original, thoroughly researched, and include practical takeaways — no heavy promotion of PilotStack beyond a natural mention where relevant.

Would any of these ideas work for [Blog Name]? Happy to adjust the angle based on what you think would resonate best with your readers.

Best,

[Your Name]
Founder, PilotStack

## General Outreach Best Practices

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Send Day** | Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (highest open rates) |
| **Send Time** | 6:00-8:00 AM or 12:00-2:00 PM recipient timezone |
| **Follow-up 1** | 3 business days after initial send |
| **Follow-up 2** | 7 business days after initial send |
| **Follow-up 3** | 14 business days after initial (final) |
| **Open Rate Goal** | >50% |
| **Reply Rate Goal** | >15% |
| **Conversion Goal** | >5% (link earned or guest post accepted) |

**Tracking:** Use tools like HubSpot Sales Hub, Mixmax, or Mailtrack for open/click tracking. Always A/B test subject lines.

**Personalization Checklist:**
- [ ] Recipient's first name in greeting
- [ ] Reference to specific content they created
- [ ] Mention of their publication/podcast by name
- [ ] Tailored value proposition (not generic)
- [ ] Low-commitment CTA (not asking for too much upfront)
- [ ] PS with additional social proof if relevant

**Follow-up Sequence Template:**

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [First Name],

Just bumping this to the top of your inbox in case you missed it. I know things get busy!

[Shortened pitch — 2 sentences max]

Happy to make this as easy as possible on your end.

Best,
[Name]
```

> **Note:** Always check `{/unsubscribe}` and `{opt-out}` links are present in compliance with CAN-SPAM and GDPR regulations. Maintain an organized outreach spreadsheet tracking: contact name, URL, template used, send date, follow-up dates, status, and notes.
